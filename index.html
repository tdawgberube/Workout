      How a circuit works. Go down the list in order, one move after the next with only 15–30 seconds between them. That's one round. After the last move, rest 90 sec to 2 min, then repeat. Do 3 rounds to start, build to 4. The moves alternate muscle groups (push/pull upstairs, squat/hinge downstairs) on purpose — while one muscle works the other recovers, so you keep moving without truly resting.

      Two of you, lifting together. Share each station: one does the move while the other rests, then trade and move on together. That swap is your built-in rest and keeps the pace honest. Only the bench-tagged move needs the bench, so it's never double-booked — whoever's not on it starts the next station.

      Pick weights you can move. A circuit lives or dies on not stopping. Choose a load where the last couple reps are tough but your form holds for the whole round. If you're failing mid-round, it's too heavy for a circuit — drop it.

      Progress it. When you can finish all 3–4 rounds at the top of the rep range with clean form, add a round, add reps, or bump 5 lb. Small steps.

      Eating still matters most. For size, eat in a surplus with ~1g protein per lb of bodyweight and sleep 8+ hours. The circuit brings the conditioning and the muscle stimulus; food and sleep build what shows up.
