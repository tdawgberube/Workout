      How To Run It

      Read once

      The Cycle — 2 On, 2 Off

      Day 1UPPER
      Day 2LOWER
      Day 3OFF
      Day 4OFF

      Train two days, rest two, then start over — the cycle rolls forward no matter what day of the week it lands on. That's about 3–4 sessions a week with two full recovery days each round.

      How a circuit works. Go down the list in order, one move after the next with only 15–30 seconds between them. That's one round. After the last move, rest 90 sec to 2 min, then repeat. Do 3 rounds to start, build to 4. The list runs heaviest moves first down to the lightest, so you set the dumbbells once for the heavy block and step down as you go — less time swapping plates, more time moving.

      Pick weights you can move. A circuit lives or dies on not stopping. Choose a load where the last couple reps are tough but your form holds for the whole round. If you're failing mid-round, it's too heavy for a circuit — drop it.

      Progress it. When you can finish all 3–4 rounds at the top of the rep range with clean form, add a round, add reps, or bump 5 lb. Small steps.

      Eating still matters most. For size, eat in a surplus with ~1g protein per lb of bodyweight and sleep 8+ hours. The circuit brings the conditioning and the muscle stimulus; food and sleep build what shows up.
